The freedom parameter (0–1)

Controls how much bright image areas are allowed to "eat into" QR modules as negative space. Higher freedom = more image visible, more EC budget consumed. Max safe = ~0.8 (stays under the 30% H-level recovery limit).

freedom = 0.0 — Pure color mosaic

All dark modules rendered. Light image areas become lightly-tinted dots. No negative space. Maximum scannability, minimum image legibility.

freedom = 0.5 — Balanced

Modules where image luminance > 50% are skipped (negative space). The logo shape becomes visible as white space within the colored dot field. Uses ~15% of the EC budget. Good balance of image legibility and scannability.

freedom = 0.8 — Maximum image

Modules where luminance > 20% are skipped. The logo dominates — its silhouette and colors are very clear. Uses ~25% of EC budget (near the H-level limit). Riskiest scannability; may need verification step.

How the budget cap works

The algorithm tracks how many dark modules have been skipped. At any freedom value, skipping is capped at 25% of total dark modules — once the budget is spent, remaining bright modules are rendered with their sampled color instead of being omitted. This keeps the code within the H-level 30% recovery threshold.
